Risk Analysis - Beta
     

A - Project Management and Governance
Has consideration been put in the current project scope and been well defined? Have sufficient processes been put in place to minimize the lack of accountability when things go wrong? Does the project have a selection of stakeholders which accurately represent the project? Are there any gaps of key roles at any level of the project? Have schedules been completed? Are the milestones and deadlines in place realistic and achievable? Has there been minimal turnover in leadership and staff positions?

B - Project Development and Training
Are there actions in place to ensure no development is continued during a code freeze? Are there processes in place to ensure decisions are not based off optimism? Is there processes/plans to give end users adequate training prior to the go-live?

C - Implementation / Go-Live
Have you ensured all requirements have been completed prior to the rollout? Has the implementation strategy been reviewed and chosen to be most appropriate? Has there been consideration into support for end users after Rollout? Are the phases of implementation identified in planning being followed in rollout? Has a plan been developed to ensure no risks are carried into the Go-Live?

D - Planning & Requirements
Has appropriate research been done for selection of vendor? Has enough planning been done for this size of project? Including goals, phases, and deadlines? Have all user requirements been laid out and assessed? Are the current negotiations between client and vendor clear and suitable for long term agreement?

E - Project Finance
Have measures been put in place to ensure the budget remains on track? Has the budget been properly reviewed by personnel with experience in similar projects? Is there processes in place to mitigate potential delays and the associated costs?

F - Communication & Collaboration
Do team members know what their role and responsibilities consist of at all project levels? Is there processes in place to ensure there has been a continued presence by the top-level in the project? Is there transparency between client, vendor, team members, and end users? Are end users being involved during the project phases as a form of feedback and training?
